📌 What Is Stock Trading?

Stock trading is the buying and selling of company shares on stock exchanges with the aim of profiting from price movements. When you buy a share, you're purchasing a small piece of ownership in a company, which entitles you to a portion of the company's assets and earnings.

Unlike long-term investors who buy and hold stocks for years, traders typically focus on shorter timeframes, capitalizing on price fluctuations driven by market sentiment, news events, and technical indicators.

💰
Capital Appreciation

Profit from rising stock prices by buying low and selling high, the most common way traders make money.

📊
Dividend Income

Earn periodic payments from companies that distribute a portion of their profits to shareholders.

⚖️
Risk Management

Successful trading requires balancing potential profit against risk through proper position sizing and stop-loss orders.

📊 Types of Stock Trading

Type Description Holding Period Risk Level Suitable For
Day Trading Buy & sell securities within the same trading day, focusing on short-term price movements Minutes to hours High High 🚀 Full-time traders with quick decision-making abilities
Swing Trading Capitalize on short to medium-term price trends and "swings" in the market Days to weeks Medium Medium 🔄 Part-time traders with day jobs
Position Trading Hold positions for extended periods to capture longer-term trends Months to years Low Low 🏦 Patient traders with fundamental analysis skills
Scalping Make numerous trades to profit from tiny price movements, often using high leverage Seconds to minutes Very High Very High ⚡ Experienced traders with advanced technology
Algorithmic Trading Use computer programs and mathematical models to execute trades at optimal times Variable Medium to High Medium 🔄 Quantitative analysts and programmers

📈 How Stocks Are Traded

🏢

Stock Exchanges

Organized marketplaces where stocks are bought and sold, including:

  • New York Stock Exchange (NYSE)
  • NASDAQ
  • London Stock Exchange (LSE)
  • Tokyo Stock Exchange (TSE)
🏛️

Brokerage Firms

Financial institutions that facilitate trading by executing orders on behalf of clients:

  • Robinhood - Commission-free mobile trading
  • TD Ameritrade - Full-service with robust tools
  • Interactive Brokers - Advanced platform for serious traders
  • Fidelity - Balanced offering for investors
📝

Order Types

Different instructions for executing trades:

  • Market Order - Execute immediately at current price
  • Limit Order - Execute only at specified price or better
  • Stop Order - Becomes market order when price reaches trigger
  • Stop-Limit Order - Becomes limit order when price reaches trigger

Trading Hours & Sessions

Stock markets operate during specific hours, with US markets typically open from 9:30 AM to 4:00 PM Eastern Time. However, extended trading hours exist:

Session Times (Eastern) Characteristics
Pre-Market 4:00 AM - 9:30 AM Lower volume, higher volatility, wider spreads
Regular Market 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM Highest volume, most liquidity
After Hours 4:00 PM - 8:00 PM Lower volume, higher volatility, news-driven moves

📊 Important Stock Metrics

Successful traders analyze various metrics to make informed decisions. These key indicators help evaluate a stock's value, growth potential, and risk.

Price Metrics

▼
Metric Description Why It Matters
Last Price Current trading price of the stock Shows stock value at that moment; basis for calculating gains/losses
Previous Close Final price at which stock traded on previous day Used to calculate daily price change and performance
52-Week Range Lowest & highest price in the past year Measures volatility & provides context for current price
Moving Averages Average price over specified periods (e.g., 50-day, 200-day) Identify trend direction and potential support/resistance levels

Valuation Metrics

▼
Metric Description Why It Matters
Market Cap Total value of all outstanding shares Indicates company size, stability, and market category
P/E Ratio Price divided by earnings per share Shows how expensive a stock is relative to its earnings
PEG Ratio P/E ratio divided by earnings growth rate Factors in growth; lower values indicate potential undervaluation
Price-to-Book (P/B) Price divided by book value per share Compares market value to accounting value; below 1 may indicate undervaluation
Price-to-Sales (P/S) Price divided by sales per share Useful for evaluating unprofitable companies or comparing within sectors

Financial Performance Metrics

▼
Metric Description Why It Matters
EPS Earnings Per Share - net income divided by outstanding shares Higher EPS indicates better profitability per share
ROE Return on Equity - net income divided by shareholder equity Measures how efficiently company uses equity to generate profit
Profit Margin Net income divided by revenue Reflects company's ability to convert revenue into profit
Dividend & Yield Payout per share & percentage return based on price Indicates income potential from stock ownership

Trading & Volatility Metrics

▼
Metric Description Why It Matters
Volume Number of shares traded in a given period High volume indicates strong interest and liquidity
Avg. Volume 3-month average daily trading volume Provides baseline to compare current trading activity
Beta Stock's volatility compared to the market (1 = equal) >1 = more volatile than market, <1 = less volatile
RSI Relative Strength Index - momentum oscillator (0-100) Above 70 = potentially overbought, below 30 = potentially oversold
ATR Average True Range - measure of volatility Higher values indicate more volatility; useful for setting stop-losses

📊 Stock Market Participants

The market consists of various participants, each with different goals, resources, and strategies:

👨‍💼

Individual Investors

Retail traders investing personal capital with varying degrees of experience and capital. They typically trade through online brokerage platforms.

🏢

Institutional Investors

Large organizations managing substantial portfolios including mutual funds, pension funds, insurance companies, and Hedgex funds.

⚖️

Market Makers

Financial firms that provide liquidity by continuously buying and selling securities, maintaining an orderly market and profiting from the bid-ask spread.

🏛️

Brokers

Entities that execute trades on behalf of clients, offering various services from basic order execution to comprehensive research and advice.

💹

High-Frequency Traders

Specialized firms using advanced algorithms and technology to execute large numbers of trades in fractions of a second, capitalizing on tiny price discrepancies.

Trading Strategies

Different approaches to trading stocks based on various methodologies and timeframes.

Technical Analysis Strategies

Strategies based on chart patterns, indicators, and historical price data:

📈

Trend Following

Identifying and trading in the direction of established market trends using moving averages, trendlines, and momentum indicators.

  • Moving Average Crossovers
  • MACD Strategy
  • Trend Channel Trading
↕️

Range Trading

Buying at support levels and selling at resistance levels in sideways markets, using oscillators like RSI and Stochastic.

  • Support/Resistance Bounces
  • Bollinger Band Strategies
  • Overbought/Oversold Indicators
🔄

Breakout Trading

Entering trades when price breaks through significant support or resistance levels with increased volume.

  • Chart Pattern Breakouts
  • Triangle and Flag Patterns
  • Volume Confirmation

Fundamental Analysis Strategies

Strategies based on company financials, industry trends, and economic factors:

💰

Value Investing

Finding undervalued stocks based on financial metrics and intrinsic value calculations.

  • Low P/E, P/B Ratio Screening
  • Discounted Cash Flow Analysis
  • Margin of Safety Principle
🚀

Growth Investing

Focusing on companies with strong revenue and earnings growth potential, often trading at higher multiples.

  • Revenue Growth Analysis
  • Earnings Momentum Strategy
  • Market Share Expansion
📰

Event-Driven Trading

Capitalizing on significant events like earnings announcements, mergers, or product launches.

  • Earnings Surprise Strategy
  • Merger Arbitrage
  • FDA Approval Trading

Hybrid & Alternative Strategies

Combining multiple approaches or utilizing specialized methodologies:

⚖️

Pairs Trading

Taking opposite positions in two correlated securities to profit from the convergence of their price relationship.

🔄

Rotational Trading

Moving between different sectors or asset classes based on economic cycles and relative strength.

🤖

Algorithm-Based

Using automated systems with predefined rules to execute trades based on technical and/or fundamental criteria.

Analysis Methods

Different ways to evaluate stocks and make informed trading decisions.

Technical Analysis

The study of historical price and volume data to predict future price movements using charts and statistical indicators.

Chart Patterns

▼

Visual patterns that form on price charts that may indicate continuation or reversal of trends.

  • Head and Shoulders - Reversal pattern with three peaks, the middle being highest
  • Double Top/Bottom - Reversal pattern showing two peaks/troughs at similar levels
  • Cup and Handle - Continuation pattern resembling a teacup with handle
  • Triangles - Consolidation patterns showing converging trendlines (ascending, descending, symmetric)
  • Flags and Pennants - Short-term continuation patterns after strong price moves

Technical Indicators

▼

Mathematical calculations based on price, volume, or open interest used to forecast future price movements.

Indicator Type Examples Purpose
Trend Indicators Moving Averages, MACD, ADX Identify direction and strength of trends
Momentum Oscillators RSI, Stochastic, CCI Measure overbought/oversold conditions
Volatility Indicators Bollinger Bands, ATR, Keltner Channels Gauge market volatility and potential breakouts
Volume Indicators On-Balance Volume, Volume Profile, VWAP Confirm price movements with volume analysis

Fundamental Analysis

Evaluating a company's financial health, business model, competitive position, and growth prospects to determine its intrinsic value.

Financial Statement Analysis

▼

Examining key financial documents to assess company performance:

  • Income Statement - Revenue, expenses, and profitability
  • Balance Sheet - Assets, liabilities, and shareholder equity
  • Cash Flow Statement - Cash generation and usage
  • Financial Ratios - Metrics derived from financial statements

Qualitative Analysis

▼

Assessing non-financial aspects that impact company value:

  • Business Model - How the company generates revenue
  • Competitive Advantage - Unique strengths and moats
  • Management Quality - Leadership track record and vision
  • Industry Position - Market share and growth potential
  • Regulatory Environment - Legal and compliance factors

Trading Psychology

The study of how emotions and cognitive biases influence trading decisions and performance.

Common Trading Biases

Psychological tendencies that can negatively impact trading performance:

😨

Fear & Greed

The two primary emotions driving market movements and individual trading decisions:

  • Fear leads to panic selling, staying out of markets, or abandoning strategies
  • Greed causes overtrading, taking excessive risks, and holding winners too long
🔄

Confirmation Bias

Seeking information that supports existing beliefs while ignoring contradictory evidence.

Example: Only reading bullish articles when you own a stock, dismissing negative news.

💸

Loss Aversion

The tendency to feel losses more strongly than equivalent gains, leading to poor risk management.

Example: Holding losing positions too long hoping for recovery while taking profits too early.

🔮

Recency Bias

Giving excessive weight to recent events and extrapolating short-term trends too far.

Example: Assuming a stock that recently surged will continue rising indefinitely.

Developing Mental Discipline

Strategies to improve psychological resilience and decision-making quality:

📝

Trading Plan

Creating a detailed, written strategy with specific entry, exit, and risk management rules.

A good plan defines exactly when to trade, what to trade, how much to risk, and when to exit.

📊

Trading Journal

Documenting trades, including rationale, outcomes, and emotional states to identify patterns.

Regular review helps identify both technical and psychological improvement areas.

⏸️

Mindfulness

Developing awareness of emotional states and learning to pause before acting on impulses.

Techniques like meditation and deep breathing can help maintain calm during market volatility.

👨‍💻

Deliberate Practice

Focused, systematic improvement of specific trading skills with regular feedback.

Includes backtesting strategies, simulation trading, and post-trade analysis.
